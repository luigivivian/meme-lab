## Summary

Comprehensive research into Sora 2 prompt engineering techniques from the official OpenAI Cookbook, the awesome-sora2 community repository, and multiple practitioner guides reveals a well-documented set of patterns that can significantly improve the current VideoPromptBuilder templates. The existing 17 MOTION_TEMPLATES use a decent subject-action-first approach but lack three critical dimensions that modern Sora 2 prompting requires: (1) a three-layer motion hierarchy (macro camera, mid subject, micro environment), (2) explicit temporal sequencing with present continuous tense, and (3) physics-aware material/force descriptions for cloth, particles, and lighting.

The current system prompt caps output at 300 chars and instructs "2-4 sentences" -- research confirms that 300-500 chars with 4-5 sentences is the sweet spot for i2v, matching the D-04 and D-07 decisions. The biggest quality gain will come from restructuring templates to separate camera direction from character animation and adding a physics layer, plus adopting present continuous tense ("is slowly raising" vs "raises") which Sora 2 interprets as ongoing motion rather than one-shot events.

**Primary recommendation:** Rewrite all 17 MOTION_TEMPLATES using the three-layer motion framework (macro/mid/micro), present continuous tense, and explicit physics descriptors. Restructure _SYSTEM_PROMPT with the structured section format (Camera / Subject / Physics / Style) that the OpenAI Cookbook recommends.

### Pattern 1: Three-Layer Motion Framework
**What:** Every motion prompt must address three distinct motion scales:
- **Macro** (camera movement): `"Slow push-in from medium shot"`
- **Mid** (subject action): `"Wizard is slowly raising his staff with both hands"`
- **Micro** (environment details): `"Dust particles are drifting upward in the light beam, robe fabric is swaying gently"`

**When to use:** Every MOTION_TEMPLATE and every LLM-generated prompt.

**Why:** OpenAI Cookbook and community consensus: "A prompt that covers all three levels produces far more cinematic results than a single-scale description." Source: OpenAI Sora 2 Prompting Guide + fal.ai guide + Artlist blog.

**Example:**
```python
# Source: OpenAI Cookbook + awesome-sora2 community patterns
# Pattern: [macro camera] + [mid subject] + [micro environment]
MOTION_TEMPLATES_V2["sabedoria"] = (
    "Slow push-in from medium shot, static camera with subtle parallax. "
    "The wizard is gently stroking his long beard with one hand, head nodding slowly in contemplation. "
    "His other hand is gripping the staff as the crystal tip pulses with a steady blue glow. "
    "Robes are swaying softly with his breathing, magical particles are drifting upward around him, "
    "warm golden rim light catches the fabric edges."
)
```

### Pattern 2: Present Continuous Tense for Ongoing Motion
**What:** Use present continuous ("is raising", "are drifting") instead of simple present ("raises", "drift") for all motion descriptions.

**When to use:** All v2 templates and system prompt instructions.

**Why:** "Present-tense, continuous language helps Sora 2 interpret the prompt as ongoing motion rather than a one-time event." Source: WaveSpeedAI blog, community consensus.

**Example:**
```python
# BAD (v1 pattern - interpreted as single event):
"The wizard raises his staff triumphantly"

# GOOD (v2 pattern - interpreted as continuous motion):
"The wizard is raising his staff triumphantly, arm extending upward"
```

### Pattern 3: Structured System Prompt with Sections
**What:** Organize system prompt instructions into labeled sections matching the three-layer framework.

**When to use:** _SYSTEM_PROMPT and _ENHANCE_PROMPT restructuring.

**Why:** OpenAI Cookbook: "Structure your prompt with clear sections rather than single paragraphs to reduce ambiguity."

**Example:**
```python
_SYSTEM_PROMPT_V2 = (
    "You generate motion prompts for Sora 2 image-to-video AI. "
    "You receive an image of a cartoon wizard character and must describe the animation.\n\n"
    "OUTPUT FORMAT (4-5 sentences, 300-500 chars):\n"
    "1. CAMERA: One clear camera movement (static, slow push-in, gentle pan, subtle parallax)\n"
    "2. SUBJECT: Primary character animation — specific body movements using present continuous tense "
    "(is raising, is turning, are gripping). Include micro-actions: breathing, blinking, weight shifts\n"
    "3. PHYSICS: Cloth behavior (robes swaying, beard flowing), particle effects, material interactions\n"
    "4. ATMOSPHERE: Lighting shifts, ambient motion (wind, sparkles, glow pulses)\n\n"
    "CRITICAL RULES:\n"
    "- Use PRESENT CONTINUOUS tense (is walking, are floating, is glowing)\n"
    "- ONE camera movement per prompt — do not combine pan + zoom + track\n"
    "- CHARACTER is PRIMARY subject — background is secondary ambient motion\n"
    "- Describe motion in BEATS: initial state -> peak action -> settling\n"
    "- Include force/material descriptors: 'silk-like robe fabric', 'heavy wooden staff'\n"
    "- NO speech, NO dialogue, NO lip movement, NO text\n"
    "- NO scene changes, NO new objects appearing\n"
    "- Maintain cartoon cel-shading art style\n"
    "- Portrait 4:5 aspect ratio\n"
    "- NEVER describe character appearance (already in the image)"
)
```

### Anti-Patterns to Avoid
- **Multiple camera movements in one prompt:** "Pan left while zooming in and tracking upward" -- Sora 2 struggles to prioritize. Use ONE camera move per shot.
- **Vague descriptors:** "beautiful", "cinematic", "epic" -- replace with specific terms like "warm golden rim light", "shallow depth of field", "soft diffused lighting".
- **Simple present tense for motion:** "wizard raises staff" -- Sora 2 treats this as a completed action rather than ongoing animation. Use "wizard is raising staff".
- **Overloading prompts:** Too many simultaneous actions cause morphing/jitter. One camera + one primary subject action + ambient detail.
- **Generic camera instructions:** "camera moves slowly" -- be specific: "slow dolly-in from medium shot" or "static camera with subtle parallax from staff".

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Camera + Subject Movement Conflict
**What goes wrong:** Describing a complex camera move AND significant subject movement simultaneously causes Sora 2 to produce jittery, confused output.
**Why it happens:** The model cannot prioritize two competing motion instructions.
**How to avoid:** Use ONE camera move + ONE primary subject action. Camera should be simple (static or slow push-in) to let subject animation shine.
**Warning signs:** Generated video shows unstable framing or character morphing.

### Pitfall 2: Simple Present Tense Produces Static Results
**What goes wrong:** "The wizard strokes his beard" generates a brief single-frame gesture instead of continuous motion.
**Why it happens:** Simple present implies completed action; Sora 2 treats it as a one-time event.
**How to avoid:** Always use present continuous: "The wizard is stroking his beard, fingers running through the white strands."
**Warning signs:** Character performs action in first 1-2 seconds then freezes.

### Pitfall 3: Prompt Length Sweet Spot Violation
**What goes wrong:** Too short (<200 chars) gives generic results; too long (>600 chars) causes the model to ignore parts.
**Why it happens:** Short prompts lack specificity; long prompts overwhelm the instruction-following capacity in 10s clips.
**How to avoid:** Target 300-500 chars (4-5 sentences) per D-04/D-07. Each sentence serves a specific layer.
**Warning signs:** Generated video ignores later sentences or adds unexpected elements.

### Pitfall 4: Inconsistent Physics Descriptions
**What goes wrong:** Describing cloth behavior without specifying material/force produces uncanny floating effects.
**Why it happens:** Sora 2 needs material hints to simulate physics correctly.
**How to avoid:** Specify material + force: "silk-like robe fabric billowing in gentle breeze" not just "robes move".
**Warning signs:** Cloth looks like rigid cardboard or zero-gravity floating.

### Pitfall 5: Describing Character Appearance in i2v Prompt
**What goes wrong:** Repeating character details ("old wizard with blue robes and white beard") in an i2v prompt causes the model to try re-rendering the character, potentially changing the original image.
**Why it happens:** i2v anchors to the input image for appearance; text should only describe MOTION.
**How to avoid:** Never describe appearance in motion prompts -- only describe what CHANGES (movement, physics, lighting).
**Warning signs:** Character's appearance shifts or clothes change color during video.
